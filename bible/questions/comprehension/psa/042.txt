====== Psalms Chapter 042 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? How does the writer's soul pant after God?

A. His soul pants after God as the deer pants after streams of water. [42:1]

Q? What does the psalmist's soul do?

A. His soul thirsts for God, for the living God, and asks when he will come and appear before God. [42:2]

Q? What have his tears been?

A. His tears have been his food day and night. [42:3]

Q? What things does the psalmist call to mind?

A. He calls to mind how he went with the throng and led them to the house of God with the voice of joy and praise. [42:4]

Q? What does the psalmist suggest doing rather than his soul melting away or worrying?

A. He should hope in God, for he will yet praise God for the help of God's presence. [42:5]

Q? What does David do because his soul has melted away within himself?

A. He calls God to mind from the land of the Jordan, from the three peaks of Mount Hermon and from the hill of Mizar. [42:6]

Q? What has gone over the psalmist?

A. All God's waves and his billows have gone over the psalmist. [42:7]

Q? What will Yahweh do in the daytime?

A. In the daytime, he will command his covenant faithfulness. [42:8]

Q? What will happen in the night?

A. In the night God's song will be with the psalmist, a prayer to the God of his life. [42:8]

Q? What will the psalmist say to God, his rock?

A. He will ask God why God has forgotten him and why does the psalmist go mourning because of the oppression of the enemy. [42:9]

Q? To what does the psalmist compare his adversaries' rebukes when they ask him where his God is?

A. They are compared to a sword in his bones. [42:10]

Q? What will the psalmist do instead of his soul melting away or being worried within himself?

A. He will hope in God, for he will yet praise God, who is the help of his face and his God. [42:13]
