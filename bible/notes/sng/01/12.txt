===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **[The woman speaking to herself]** - (See: [[:vi:ta:vol2:translate:figs_apostrophe]])
  * **nard emitted its fragrance** - Oil was extracted from the expensive nard or spikenard (valerian plant with small pink or white flowers) and was used to make the skin soft and give the woman a pleasant odor.
  * **emitted** - "gave off," "breathed," or "spilled"
  * **My beloved is to me like a bag of myrrh** - In that culture women put a small amount of expensive myrrh between their breasts to give them a continual pleasant odor. She is describing how she feels about the man: she thinks about him all the time with delight and passion. (See: [[:vi:ta:vol1:translate:figs_simile]])
  * **My beloved is like...henna flowers in the vineyards of En Gedi** - The woman is saying that her lover is like flowers from a famous oasis (springs in a desert) along the western shore of the Dead Sea known for their beautiful vineyards and fragrant flowers. 
  * **henna flowers** - flowers from a small desert tree
