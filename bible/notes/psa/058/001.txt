===== Translation Notes: =====

David wrote this song for the chief musician.

  * **Al Tashheth** - See how you translated this in 57:1.
  * **michtam** - See how you translated this in 16:1.
  * **Do you indeed in silence speak righteousness **  - The psalmist, David, is using this question to rebuke the mighty men who are judges. AT: " When you mighty men speak, you never say what is right." (See: [[:vi:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]])
  * **Do you judge uprightly, you sons of men **  - The psalmist, David, is using this question to emphasize that the mighty men judge unjustly. AT: "you never justly decide things about people." (See: [[:vi:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]])
  * **you work wickedness in your heart **- AT: "Your plans are to do what is wrong." (See [[:vi:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **you weigh out the violence of your hands to the earth. **- The psalmist says the judges are not choosing justice but are working violence instead. AT: "You choose violence over justice." (See: [[:vi:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
